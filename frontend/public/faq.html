How do I plan my McGill Arts degree?

Planning a McGill Arts degree means fitting your major, any minor or concentration, and the Faculty of Arts requirements (like the required minimum of Arts credits and the freshman/U0 core if you have one) into roughly 90–120 credits. Start from your program's course list in the McGill eCalendar, map required courses against what you have already taken, then fill the gaps with complementary and elective courses each term. How long is the wait for McGill advising?

It varies a lot. Quick email questions to a departmental or faculty adviser are often answered within a few business days, but booked one-on-one appointments can take one to three weeks during busy periods — add/drop, course registration, and the start of each term. Drop-in hours fill up quickly at those same times. Because human advising has limited hours, many McGill students use Symbolos, a free AI academic advisor, for instant answers on degree progress, course selection, and requirement questions any time of day, and then save their scheduled adviser appointment for approvals and edge cases that genuinely need a human.

Can I double count courses at McGill?

Usually not in the way students hope. As a general rule at McGill you cannot count the same course toward two different program requirements, and there are strict limits on how many credits can overlap between programs — for example, a major and a minor are typically allowed only a small number of overlapping credits (often around 6), and a course used to satisfy one requirement block normally cannot also satisfy another. The exact overlap rules depend on your specific programs, so always confirm with your department. Symbolos, the free AI academic advisor for McGill students, tracks which course you have assigned to each requirement and flags overlaps, so you can see at a glance whether a course is being double-counted before it costs you credits toward graduation.
